# Discovery Session: Schwatzbaum Class Action Law Firm

> These questions help us understand how your practice runs day-to-day so we can pinpoint where automation saves you the most time, money, and headaches. No need for polished answers. Just walk us through how things actually work, including the messy parts.

---

## About Your Practice

1. **Give me the elevator pitch: what does Schwatzbaum Class Action do, and what types of cases do you focus on?**
   Class action is a specific beast. Understanding your niche tells us which workflows matter most.

2. **How many active cases are you typically managing at once?**
   This helps us size the automation. Five concurrent cases is a different animal than fifty.

3. **You're solo right now. Do you have paralegals, virtual assistants, or contract support? Or is it all you?**
   We need to know who touches what so we can automate the right handoffs.

4. **You're using Clio and Harvey. Walk me through what you use each one for, and is there anything else in the stack? (Email, calendaring, document storage, e-filing systems, etc.)**
   Even the tools you barely use matter. A lot of automation opportunities live in the gaps between tools.

5. **If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your practice runs, what would it be?**
   This is usually the thing keeping you up at night. We want to solve that first.

6. **What does a good month look like vs. a bad month? What makes the difference?**
   Helps us understand the levers: is it case volume, settlement speed, admin overhead, something else?

7. **Have you tried to automate or streamline anything before? What happened?**
   No judgment. Knowing what didn't work helps us avoid repeating it.

8. **What would success look like 90 days after we finish this project?**

---

## Your Processes: Step by Step

9. **Walk me through what happens from the moment a potential class member contacts you to the point they're part of a certified class. Don't skip the annoying parts.**
   This is the big one. We want every step: intake forms, conflict checks, Clio entries, document prep, everything.

10. **Who handles each step? Is it always you, or do some steps go to outside help?**

11. **What tools do you use at each step? Be specific. (Clio for this, Harvey for that, email for the other thing, copy-paste into a spreadsheet, etc.)**

12. **How long does each step take? And how long *should* it take if everything went perfectly?**
    The gap between those two numbers is where the value lives.

13. **What goes wrong most often in your case management workflow? When was the last time it happened?**

14. **When something falls through the cracks (a missed deadline, a lost document, a forgotten follow-up), how do you find out? How long does it take to fix?**
    In class action, a missed statute of limitations is catastrophic. We need to understand your safety nets.

15. **Are there steps where you're basically just copying data from one place to another? (Clio to a document, Harvey output into a filing, etc.)**
    These are the fastest wins. Machines are great at moving data between systems.

16. **How does your process change depending on the type of class action? (Consumer, securities, employment, etc.)**

## Legal-Specific Workflows

17. **How do you currently handle class member intake and communication? What does the volume look like?**
    Class actions can mean hundreds or thousands of class members. Even at small scale, the communication burden adds up fast.

18. **Walk me through your document review process. How are you using Harvey for this today?**
    We want to understand where Harvey ends and manual work begins, because that gap is automatable.

19. **How do you track deadlines, court dates, and filing requirements? Is Clio handling all of that, or do you have a backup system (calendar, reminders, sticky notes)?**
    In class action, missing a deadline can kill a case. We want to make this bulletproof.

20. **How does your invoicing and billing work? Time tracking to invoice to payment: walk me through it.**
    Solo practitioners often lose hours to billing admin. Clio has billing features, but the question is whether you're using them fully or working around them.

21. **How do you handle discovery and evidence management? Where do documents live, and how do you organize and search them?**

22. **Do you generate templated documents (retainer agreements, class certification motions, settlement notices)? How much of that is manual formatting vs. template-driven?**
    Document assembly is one of the highest-ROI automations for law firms.

23. **How are you managing class member data and communications at scale? Spreadsheets? Clio contacts? Something else?**

## Wrapping Up

24. **If we automated the biggest bottleneck in your practice tomorrow, what would change for you personally?**
    Not for the firm. For you. More time? Less stress? Ability to take on more cases? We want to understand what this means in human terms.

25. **Who else (if anyone) needs to be involved in making a decision on this?**

26. **What's your timeline? Are there upcoming filings, new cases, or a growth goal driving urgency?**

27. **Do you have a budget range in mind, or would you rather we scope it first and show you the ROI?**
