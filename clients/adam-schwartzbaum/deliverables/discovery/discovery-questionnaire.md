# Discovery Session: Adam Schwartzbaum

> These questions help us understand how your practice operates day to day and where automation will have the biggest impact on your arbitration management workflow. We don't need perfect answers. Walk us through how things actually work, including the messy parts. Everything discussed is confidential and all deliverables will account for your ethical obligations and bar rules.

## About Your Practice

1. What does your practice focus on, and how would you describe it to someone at a dinner party?

2. How many people are on your team, and who touches the arbitration management workflow today? (attorneys, paralegals, admin staff, outside counsel)

3. What's your current revenue model? (contingency, per-claimant fee, hourly, hybrid)

4. What tools and software do you use day to day? (case management, document generation, email, spreadsheets, even the ones you hate)

5. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your arbitration operations run, what would it be?

6. What does a good month look like vs. a bad month? What makes the difference?

7. Have you tried to automate or improve any of these processes before? What happened?

8. What would success look like 90 days after we finish this project?

## Your Processes

9. Walk me through what happens when a new potential claimant contacts your firm, from that first touchpoint all the way to a filed arbitration demand. Don't skip the annoying parts.

10. Who does each step? Is it always the same person, or does it depend on the case type or institution?

11. What tools do you use at each step? (Clio, Filevine, spreadsheets, Word docs, email, pen and paper)

12. How long does each step take? How long should it take?

13. What goes wrong most often? When was the last time it happened?

14. When something goes wrong (missed deadline, wrong filing, bad address), how do you find out? How long does it take to fix?

15. Are there steps where someone is basically just copying data from one place to another?

16. Is any part of this process different depending on the arbitration institution, claim type, or claimant situation?

## Mass Arbitration Operations

17. How many active arbitration cases or claimants are you managing right now, and what does your typical case volume look like month to month?

18. Walk me through how a new claimant gets from first contact to a filed arbitration demand. Where does it break down?

19. What case management tools are you using today? (Clio, Filevine, spreadsheets, custom database, etc.)

20. Which arbitration institutions do you file with (AAA, JAMS, NAM), and what does the filing process look like for each?

21. How do you currently handle deadline tracking? (answer windows, inactivity deadlines, hearing dates)

22. How are you generating demand letters, releases, and other templated documents today?

23. How do claimants get updates on their case status? How often do they reach out asking for updates?

24. What does your settlement workflow look like from offer to signed release to payment distribution?

## Wrapping Up

25. If we automated the biggest bottleneck in your arbitration operations tomorrow, what would change for you personally?

26. Who else needs to be involved in making a decision on this?

27. What's your timeline? Is there a deadline, a wave of new filings, or an event driving urgency?

28. Do you have a budget range in mind, or would you rather we scope it first and show you the ROI?
