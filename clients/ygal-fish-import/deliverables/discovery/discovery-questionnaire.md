# Discovery Session: Seven Seas International

> Ygal, these questions help us map out how Seven Seas actually runs day to day, so we can figure out exactly where automation saves you the most time and money. We already know container tracking and customs/ISF are the big ones. These questions go deeper into those and poke around the edges for anything else worth tackling.
>
> No perfect answers needed. Walk us through how things actually work, including the messy parts. Should take about 30 minutes.

## About Your Business

These give us the big picture so we can size things properly.

1. Give us the dinner-party pitch for Seven Seas. How do you describe what you do to someone who's never heard of international frozen seafood distribution?

2. How big is the team that touches logistics and operations? Who handles what across your offices and time zones?

3. What's the revenue model look like? Per-order, per-container, contract pricing with large buyers, some mix?

4. Walk us through every tool your team uses day to day. Google Sheets, carrier portals, email, WhatsApp, customs software, ERP, accounting. Even the ones you've outgrown or hate.

5. If you could snap your fingers and fix one thing about how Seven Seas operates, what would it be?

6. What separates a great month from a rough one? Is it shipping volume, customs delays, carrier issues, something else?

7. Have you tried to automate or streamline any of this before? What happened?

8. Fast-forward 90 days after we finish this project. What does success look like for you?

## Your Processes

Now we dig into how things actually work step by step. Think of it like you're handing your job off to someone for two weeks and writing down every detail they'd need.

9. Walk us through your shipment tracking process from the moment a new container is booked to the moment it's delivered. Every step, every handoff, every "then I check the carrier website again" moment.

10. Who does each step? Is it always the same person, or does it depend on the carrier, the route, or the product line?

11. What tools are involved at each step? Carrier portals, Google Sheets, email threads, phone calls, WhatsApp messages?

12. How long does each step take? How long should it take if everything went smoothly?

13. What goes wrong most often? When was the last time a missed ETA update or a customs delay caused a real problem?

14. When something goes wrong (a container gets held, an ETA changes, a customer calls asking where their shipment is), how do you find out? How long does it take to fix?

15. Are there steps where someone is basically just copying data from a carrier website into the spreadsheet, or from an email into the tracking sheet?

16. Does the process change depending on the carrier, the origin country, or the product type? For example, is tracking a Maersk container different from tracking TRIDENT domestic shipments?

## Logistics & Shipping

These get into the specifics of your shipping operation.

17. How many containers or shipments are in transit at any given time? What's the weekly or monthly volume?

18. Walk us through how a shipment gets booked. From "we need to move X from Qingdao to Seattle" to the container actually leaving port. Where are the bottlenecks?

19. You're using Maersk, MSC, Evergreen, Cosco, OOCL, and TRIDENT. How do you decide which carrier gets each shipment? Rate shopping, existing contracts, route availability?

20. How do tracking updates get to your customers? Do you send them proactively, or do customers call asking where their container is?

21. What happens when a shipment goes sideways? Container damage, wrong port, customs hold, rejected product. Walk us through a recent example.

22. How does address and destination validation work for international shipments? Do freight forwarders handle that, or does your team verify ports, warehouses, and delivery addresses?

## Customs & ISF

23. Who handles ISF filings? You mentioned that you and "C" split them. How does the split work, and what triggers a filing?

24. Beyond ISF, what other customs documents do you prepare per shipment? Bill of lading, health certificates, phytosanitary certs, certificates of origin, FDA prior notice?

25. When do customs docs get filed relative to the shipment? Before departure, during transit, at arrival? Does it vary by origin country?

26. How do you track customs status right now? Calls and emails with the broker, a portal, or just notes in the spreadsheet?

27. How often do containers get held at customs? What does a hold cost you in demurrage, delayed delivery, or lost product?

28. For the UK and Haiti shipments, do you handle customs on those routes too, or is that the buyer's responsibility?

## Wrapping Up

29. If we automated the tracking and customs updates tomorrow, what would change for you personally? What would you do with that time?

30. Who else at Seven Seas needs to be involved in making a decision on this?

31. Is there a timeline or event driving urgency? Peak shipping season, a big contract, team changes?

32. Do you have a budget range in mind, or would you rather we scope it first and show you the ROI?

33. Is there anything about this questionnaire or the process so far that we could do better?
